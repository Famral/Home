CUET 2026 Admission Guide & Insights

Delhi University (DU) CSAS Portal

DU allocates seats through the **Common Seat Allocation System (CSAS)** in three primary phases:

  • Phase 1: CSAS Registration: Apply on the official portal and upload your details and class 12th transcripts.
  • Phase 2: Preferences Entry: Select and rank courses and colleges. Arrange highly preferred options at the top. The Choice Basket on this page helps you map this.
  • Phase 3: Seat Allocation Rounds: Seats are systematically allocated based on merit scores, priority lists, and availability. Upward movement is allowed in successive rounds.

Important Admission Factors

CUET cutoffs fluctuate due to several critical parameters:

  • Normalisation Process: Raw scores from different testing shifts are converted into normalized NTA scores. Always use normalized scores in the predictor.
  • Program Specific Eligibility: DU requires custom subject mappings (e.g. English + Mathematics is compulsory for B.A. Hons Economics). Make sure your domain subject tests match course requirements.
  • Regional & State Quotas: Delhi State candidates get a reservation of 85% at Dr. B.R. Ambedkar University (AUD). Central universities (DU, BHU) do not have state reservations but support minority seats.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the predicted seat guaranteed?
No, seat allotments are governed by official university bodies (like DU CSAS, BHU Counseling) based on real-time registrations, category-wise vacancies, and locked preferences. This predictor provides logical estimates based on previous year NTA cutoff scores.
How does the predictor handle different category relaxations?
Cutoffs for OBC-NCL, EWS, SC, ST, and PwD categories are significantly relaxed compared to the Unreserved (UR) category. This tool applies appropriate historical offset point adjustments to simulate seat chances for each specific category.
How are women-only colleges handled?
Colleges like Lady Shri Ram College (LSR), Miranda House, Gargi, and Maitreyi under DU, and MMV under BHU are exclusive to female candidates. If the gender filter is set to Male/Co-Ed, these colleges are filtered out, while selecting Female unlocks these premium options.
What is the difference between CUET Score and Percentile modes?
CUET Score represents the actual normalized marks obtained out of 800. Percentile represents your relative standing among candidates. If Percentile is selected, the tool maps the percentile value to an estimated normalized score using non-linear score mappings before processing predictions.
