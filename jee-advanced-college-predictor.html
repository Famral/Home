Understanding the JoSAA Seat Allocation Process

IIT admissions are administered by the Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) through a centralized online platform. JoSAA coordinates counselling for all 23 IITs, featuring multiple seat allocation rounds. Before final allotments, mock seat allocations are published to help candidates understand their projection trends and reorder options.

IIT Fee Structure & Fee Waivers

IITs have a standard fee structure that is highly subsidized by the government, along with extensive waivers:

  • Standard Tuition Fees: Typically around Rs 2,00,000 to Rs 2,25,000 per year for General, OBC, and EWS candidates.
  • Full Fee Waiver (SC/ST/PwD): 100% tuition fee waiver is provided to SC, ST, and PwD candidates across all IITs.
  • Income-Based Fee Waivers: Candidates with family income below Rs 1 Lakh per year get a 100% tuition fee waiver. Candidates with income between Rs 1 Lakh and Rs 5 Lakhs get a 2/3rd tuition fee waiver.

Complete List of IITs & Campus Insights

There are 23 official Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) representing the pinnacle of engineering education. Here is the complete list categorized by generation to help you make informed JoSAA choices.

1st Gen IITs (Old Legacy)

  • IIT Kharagpur (Est. 1951) Located in West Bengal. India's first IIT, housing a massive 2,100-acre campus. Renowned for its strong core engineering heritage, dual degrees, and the prestigious agricultural & food technology branches.
  • IIT Bombay (Est. 1958) Located in Powai, Mumbai. Most popular choice for top-rankers. Excellent entrepreneurship cell, active industrial projects, and highly lucrative placements.
  • IIT Madras (Est. 1959) Located in Chennai. Ranked #1 consistently in NIRF. Hosts an outstanding Research Park, promoting advanced research in deep-tech startups and electronics.
  • IIT Kanpur (Est. 1959) Located in Uttar Pradesh. Renowned for its rigorous academic curriculum, computing infrastructure, and active engineering physics research departments.
  • IIT Delhi (Est. 1961) Located in Hauz Khas, New Delhi. Famous for its stellar startup ecosystem, venture capital network, and extremely competitive CSE cutoffs.
  • IIT Guwahati (Est. 1994) Located on the banks of Brahmaputra River, Assam. Highly scenic campus with strong accomplishments in biotechnology, design, and nanotechnology fields.
  • IIT Roorkee (Est. 1847) Located in Uttarakhand. The oldest engineering college in Asia (formerly Thomason College of Civil Engineering). Renowned civil, hydrology, and architectural legacy.

2nd Gen IITs (Est. 2008-2009)

  • IIT Hyderabad (Est. 2008) Located in Sangareddy, Telangana. The fastest growing 2nd-gen IIT. Strong Japanese academic partnerships, top-tier research in AI, and close ties with Hyderabad's tech sector.
  • IIT (BHU) Varanasi (Est. 1919 / IIT 2012) Located inside BHU campus, Uttar Pradesh. Rich history. Very large alumni base, excellent coding environment, and highly competitive mining and metallurgy streams.
  • IIT Indore (Est. 2009) Located in Simrol, Madhya Pradesh. Excellent coding track record in competitive programming (ACM ICPC), high research citations, and modern hostel setups.
  • IIT Gandhinagar (Est. 2008) Located in Gandhinagar, Gujarat. Award-winning eco-friendly campus. Known for custom curriculum flexibility, study-abroad program access, and humanities focus.
  • IIT Ropar (Est. 2008) Located in Rupnagar, Punjab. Research leadership in agricultural technology, water systems management, and advanced computing applications.
  • IIT Patna (Est. 2008) Located in Bihta, Bihar. Strong B.Tech placement packages, especially in software development and electronics engineering roles.
  • IIT Jodhpur (Est. 2008) Located in Karwar, Rajasthan. Advanced labs in artificial intelligence, digital humanities, and solar energy research.
  • IIT Mandi (Est. 2009) Located in Kamand Valley, Himachal Pradesh. Stunning mountain valley campus with design-driven, project-oriented learning modules.
  • IIT Bhubaneswar (Est. 2008) Located in Argul, Odisha. Sprawling green campus. Very good core department labs, wind-tunnel research facilities, and seaside engineering systems focus.

3rd Gen IITs (Est. 2015-2016)

  • IIT Dhanbad (ISM) (Est. 1926 / IIT 2016) Located in Dhanbad, Jharkhand. Iconic mining school converted to IIT. Unrivaled mineral resources, geology, and petroleum research history.
  • IIT Tirupati (Est. 2015) Located in Yerpedu, Andhra Pradesh. Set up with guidance and faculty support from IIT Madras. Growing placement base for civil and electrical streams.
  • IIT Palakkad (Est. 2015) Located in Kanjikode, Kerala. Quiet campus in the Western Ghats foothills with strong software and data sciences placement ties with Kochi.
  • IIT Bhilai (Est. 2016) Located in Kutelabhata, Chhattisgarh. Permanent campus recently operationalized. Active growth in cyber-security and defense tech fields.
  • IIT Goa (Est. 2016) Located in Farmagudi, Goa. Compact transit campus. Strong coding culture with excellent hackathon records and individual attention.
  • IIT Jammu (Est. 2016) Located in Jagti, Jammu. Scenic Himalayan location. Mentored by IIT Delhi; focuses on infrastructure safety and clean energy development.
  • IIT Dharwad (Est. 2016) Located in Dharwad, Karnataka. Proximity to Hubli-Dharwad industrial zone and tech corridor, strong core branches, and IoT labs.

IIT B.Tech Admissions, expected cutoffs & JEE Advanced Rank Insights

Securing admission into the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) requires a clear understanding of the JoSAA guidelines, reservation category seat divisions, and previous closing rank trends from official JoSAA reports.

Top Indian Institutes of Technology

IITs are categorized into three generations, each with its unique advantages and cutoff patterns:

  • Old IITs (1st Gen): Establishments like IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kanpur, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Roorkee, and IIT Guwahati. These represent the pinnacle of engineering education in India, where top branches like CSE close within the top 500 CRL ranks.
  • Second Generation IITs: Established around 2008-2009, including IIT Hyderabad, IIT BHU Varanasi, IIT Indore, IIT Gandhinagar, IIT Ropar, IIT Patna, IIT Jodhpur, and IIT Bhubaneswar. These have rapidly built world-class campuses and achieve exceptional placements.
  • Third Generation IITs (Newer): Founded around 2015-2016, including IIT Tirupati, IIT Palakkad, IIT Dharwad, and IIT Jammu. These offer state-of-the-art research labs, excellent mentor-mentee ratios under the guidance of older IITs, and lower cutoff ranks.

JoSAA Seat Allocation Factors

Closing ranks vary dynamically during counselling based on these key factors:

  • Category Seat Quotas: JoSAA implements reservation for OBC-NCL (27%), SC (15%), ST (7.5%), GEN-EWS (10%), and PwD (5% in each category), besides supernumerary female-only seats (20%).
  • JEE Advanced CRL Rank: All seat allotments are strictly based on the Common Rank List (CRL) or Category Rank compiled from candidates' performance in JEE Advanced Paper 1 and Paper 2.
  • Choice Filling Preference Order: Candidates must order their choices strategically. If a candidate is eligible for multiple choices, JoSAA allocates the highest preferred choice. The Choice Basket on this page helps you organize your choices.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the JEE Advanced College Predictor and how does it work?
The JEE Advanced College Predictor is a free tool that estimates your B.Tech admission chances in top Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). Enter your JEE Advanced CRL rank or marks, select your reservation category and preferred branch, and the tool compares your rank against previous years' official closing ranks to show High, Medium, or Borderline admission probabilities.
What is the difference between CRL Rank and Category Rank in JEE Advanced?
The Common Rank List (CRL) rank is your overall rank among all candidates who qualified in JEE Advanced. The Category Rank is your rank within your specific reservation category (EWS, OBC-NCL, SC, ST). During JoSAA counselling, seat allocation is category-specific, but entering your CRL rank alongside your category here provides a reliable CRL-based admission projection.
Is the predicted seat guaranteed by the JEE Advanced College Predictor?
No. Actual seat allotments are determined by the official JoSAA counselling process based on live student choices, category-wise seat availability, and actual closing ranks in the current year. This tool provides indicative assessments based on historical trends. Actual closing ranks may vary each year.
How does the category reservation policy affect IIT admissions?
Under government policy, IITs reserve 15% seats for SC, 7.5% for ST, 27% for OBC-NCL, and 10% for GEN-EWS candidates. In addition, 20% supernumerary seats are allocated for female candidates. Candidates belonging to these categories benefit from higher CRL rank cutoffs, which are integrated into our predictor calculations.
What is the difference between different generations of IITs?
First-generation IITs (established before 2001, like IIT Bombay, Delhi, Madras, Kanpur, Kharagpur, Roorkee, Guwahati) have long-standing reputations, massive alumni networks, and very high cutoffs. Second-generation IITs (established 2008-2009, like Hyderabad, BHU, Indore, Patna, Gandhinagar) offer exceptional modern infrastructures and rising placements. Third-generation IITs (established after 2015, like Tirupati, Palakkad, Jammu, Dharwad) have newer permanent campuses and lower cutoffs, making them excellent choices for many candidates.
Can I change my choice preferences after locking them in JoSAA?
Once you freeze and lock your choices on the official JoSAA portal, they cannot be modified. It is highly recommended to plan your preferences carefully beforehand. Our Choice Basket allows you to reorder, sort, and export your preference list as a PDF so that you have a ready reference sheet during official choice filling.
How many college options can I enter during JoSAA registration?
There is no upper limit on the number of choices a candidate can register during the JoSAA choice filling process. Candidates are advised to add as many choices as they are genuinely interested in to maximize their chances of getting an allotment in early counselling rounds.
