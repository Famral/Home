How to Use the MET College Predictor for B.Tech Admissions

Selecting the optimal campus and specialization is critical. The MET College Predictor simplifies this process by matching your MET rank against official cutoff statistics to give branch-wise predictions.

Understanding MAHE Campuses & Tiers

  • MIT Manipal (Main Campus - Tier 1): The flagship campus with outstanding research legacy, premium placements (Avg 14.1 LPA), and excellent infrastructure. Requires top-tier ranks (under 10,000 for core IT branches).
  • MIT Bengaluru (Tier 2): Established in 2021, providing top urban tech industry connection in Yelahanka, Bengaluru. Follows the same degree guidelines as Manipal.
  • Manipal University Jaipur (MUJ - Tier 2): Known for spacious premium student hostel life, startup incubator cells, and moderate cutoffs.
  • SMIT Sikkim (Tier 3): Riverside campus in Sikkim with lower cutoffs, making it accessible to candidates with moderate ranks.

Frequently Asked Questions - MET College Predictor

Everything you need to know about the Manipal Entrance Test (MET) B.Tech admissions.

What is the MET College Predictor and how does it work?
The MET College Predictor is a free tool that estimates your B.Tech admission chances at MAHE Manipal campuses. By comparing your MET rank against historical cutoff ranks, the tool categorizes each campus and branch as High, Medium, or Low Chance.
How are the admission chances categorized?
Chances are classified as:
  • High Chance: Your rank is comfortably below the previous year's cutoff.
  • Medium Chance: Your rank is close to the cutoff, making it a target option.
  • Low/Borderline: Your rank is slightly higher than the cutoff, representing a dream or borderline option.
Is there a category-based reservation in MET?
For General Category seats under MET, admissions are based purely on MET rank merit without caste/category reservations (such as SC/ST/OBC). However, separate allocations exist for NRI and Foreign students.
