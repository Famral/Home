RTU Kota Rankings & Key Highlights

RTU Kota Detailed Fee Structure

Tuition fees are structured based on the allotment seat category (GAS vs SFS). Below is the comprehensive annual cost breakdown for B.Tech students:

Annual Academic Fees

  • GAS Subsidized Seats: ₹57,000 / Year (Includes academic tuition fees, university exam charges, and development fees).
  • SFS Self-Financed Seats: ₹77,000 / Year (Includes full tuition fees, university development charges, and general amenities).
  • TFWS (Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme): ₹15,000 / Year (100% of the tuition fee component is waived; candidates only pay basic development and exam charges).

Hostel & Mess Accommodation

  • Room Rent (Triple Seated): ₹28,000 / Year (Includes electricity, water supply, and security services).
  • Room Rent (Double Seated): ₹32,000 / Year (Subject to availability, preferred allocation for senior students).
  • Mess Charges (Fooding): Approximately ₹3,500 / Month (Cooperative mess system, billed monthly based on menu/diet).
  • Hostel Caution Money: ₹5,000 (One-time deposit, fully refundable upon check-out).

RTU Kota Admissions & REAP Guide

B.Tech admissions at University Departments RTU Kota are conducted purely through centralized counseling coordinated by REAP (Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process).

Eligibility & Seat Quota Rules

  • Home State Quota (85%): Reserved for candidates holding a valid Rajasthan Domicile. Allocations are based primarily on JEE Main CRL ranks.
  • All India Quota (15%): Open to candidates from all states outside Rajasthan. Allocations are based purely on JEE Main CRL ranks.
  • Academic Requirements: Minimum 45% aggregate in 10+2 (Class 12th) with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects, alongside Chemistry/Biotechnology/Technical Vocational subject.

Reporting Document Checklist

  • Allotment Letter: REAP Provisional Seat Allotment Letter.
  • Test Scores: JEE Main CRL scorecard or REAP Merit Certificate.
  • Certificates: Original Domicile, Caste Category (SC/ST/OBC/EWS/MBC), and Income Certificates (for TFWS seats).
  • School Records: Class 10th Certificate (DOB proof), Class 12th Marks Sheet, Transfer Certificate (TC), and Migration Certificate.

CBCS Curriculum, Exam & Student Portal

RTU Kota follows the internationally aligned Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) and provides a comprehensive online student portal for exam management, result access, and administrative tasks.

CBCS Credit System

  • L-T-P Credit Structure: Courses are structured around Lecture-Tutorial-Practical (L-T-P) credit charts, letting students customize learning with core, elective, and audit courses.
  • Bi-Annual Semesters: Semester assessments conducted twice a year (Odd and Even semester cycles) with regular back paper and revaluation windows.
  • SGPA & CGPA Grading: Performance is measured via SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) and CGPA (Cumulative GPA), aligned with national grading standards.
  • Industry Internships: Mandatory industry internship programs embedded in the curriculum enhance practical skill sets and industry readiness before graduation.

Student Portal & Results

  • Exam Form Submission: Semester exam forms, fee payments, and admit card downloads are managed online via the official student login portal at rtu.ac.in.
  • Check Results Online: Visit the official RTU exam portal (esuvidha.info), select your class code (e.g. B.Tech VIII Semester), enter Roll & Enrollment Number, and click 'Show Result'.
  • Revaluation & Copy Viewing: Answer sheet revaluation forms and payment gateways are available on the student portal shortly after semester result declarations.

Frequently Asked Questions about RTU Kota

Are the expected B.Tech seat cutoffs for RTU Kota guaranteed?
No, actual seat allotments are governed entirely by the official REAP counseling algorithm based on your rank, locked choices, vacancy status, and seat reservation rules. The expected cutoff tiers provided on this page represent statistical estimations based on historical REAP opening and closing cutoff ranks.
Can I upgrade from an SFS seat to a GAS seat after admission?
Yes. During the REAP upward sliding round and internal sliding phases conducted at the RTU Kota campus, candidates can request upgrading their seat type from SFS (Self-Financing Seat) to GAS (Government Aided Seat) in their branch if seats fall vacant.
What is the family income limit for TFWS seats at RTU Kota?
To qualify for the Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) supernumerary seats (5% per branch), the candidate's family annual income from all sources must be less than ₹8 Lakhs. Candidates must present a valid income certificate issued by competent authority during counseling.
Are hostel facilities available for SFS candidates at RTU Kota?
Yes. Hostels are allocated based on merit/rank list at the time of admission, irrespective of whether the seat is GAS or SFS. RTU Kota has multiple boys and girls hostels on campus with mess and basic amenities.
Is RTU Kota a government university?
Yes. Rajasthan Technical University (RTU) Kota is a public state university established by the Government of Rajasthan under the RTU Act of 2006. It is recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 2(f) and 12(B), and all programs carry AICTE approval.
How many colleges are affiliated with RTU Kota?
RTU Kota serves as the central affiliating body for over 130 engineering colleges throughout Rajasthan, spanning B.Tech, M.Tech, B.Arch, MBA, and MCA programs. The total enrolled student base exceeds 2.5 lakh students across all affiliated institutions.
How can I check my RTU Kota semester result online?
To check your result, visit the official RTU exam portal at esuvidha.info. Select your class code (e.g. B.Tech VIII Semester Main/Back Exam), enter your Roll Number and Enrollment Number, solve the captcha, and click 'Show Result' to view your detailed marksheet and SGPA score.
What is the CBCS system followed at RTU Kota?
RTU Kota utilizes the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS), where courses are structured around L-T-P (Lecture-Tutorial-Practical) credit charts. Students can customize learning with core subjects, open electives, and audit courses. Assessments run twice a year (Odd and Even semesters), and performance is graded using SGPA and CGPA metrics.
