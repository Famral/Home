About the DAP Rajasthan College Predictor 2026

The DAP Rajasthan College Predictor is a free, data-driven tool that helps polytechnic diploma aspirants estimate their admission chances across top Rajasthan government and private colleges based on official merit-based cutoff data.

Dual Entry Pathways

Enter either your Class 10th percentage for regular 3-year diploma programs, or your Class 12th / ITI percentage for direct 2nd-year lateral entry admissions. The tool maps both pathways against historical trends.

Official Cutoff Database

Built on verified merit data covering 12 major Rajasthan polytechnic colleges including GPC Jaipur, GPC Jodhpur, GPC Kota, GPC Udaipur, BTTI Pilani, and more. Cutoffs are tracked by category (UR/OBC/SC/ST/MBC/EWS), domicile, and TFWS quota.

Category & Domicile Aware

The predictor applies state counseling-approved cutoff offsets for your reservation category and domicile status (Home State Quota vs. All India Quota). Female candidates get appropriate quota relaxation.

Rajasthan Polytechnic Admission & DAP Cutoff Insights

Securing admission into Rajasthan's top polytechnic institutions requires a clear understanding of the merit-based counseling guidelines, reservation systems, and fees.

Top Rajasthan Polytechnic Colleges

Rajasthan offers excellent state government, aided, and private polytechnic institutions:

  • GPC Jaipur (Ramchandra Khaitan): Located in the capital city, it is highly preferred for core engineering diplomas and has competitive cutoffs closing around 75-80% for top branches.
  • Government Polytechnic Jodhpur & Kota: Highly respected, old polytechnics featuring premium workshop facilities and good industrial placements in local zones.
  • BTTI Pilani & Vidya Bhawan Udaipur: Top-tier private and aided institutions offering state-of-the-art computer and training labs, widely popular for placements.

DAP Seat Allocation Factors

Admission is purely merit-based using qualifying marks. Keep these key details in mind:

  • Class 10th Board Marks: For the 3-year diploma, seat allocation relies on the overall board aggregate. Having Science and Mathematics as subjects is mandatory.
  • Choice Preference Order: Order your colleges from highest preference to lowest. The Choice Basket tool helps structure this before copy-pasting onto the official portal.
  • GAS vs. SFS Fees: GAS seats are subsidized and cost only around ₹8,500/year in government institutions, while SFS seats cost around ₹28,000–30,000/year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the predicted polytechnic seat guaranteed?
No, allotments are determined by the Centre for Electronic Governance (CEG) counseling board based on live registrations and choices locked in the current year. This tool provides indicative assessments based on historical trends.
What is the difference between GAS and SFS seats in Polytechnic?
GAS stands for Government Aided Seats, which are heavily subsidized and cost around ₹8,500 per year. SFS stands for Self-Financed Seats, which have higher fees (typically ₹28,000 - ₹32,000 per year) and are offered in private colleges or self-financed branches of government colleges.
Does the gender setting affect chances in DAP?
Yes, Rajasthan polytechnic institutions reserve horizontal supernumerary quotas for female candidates. This generally results in slightly lower cutoff thresholds for female candidates in government colleges.
