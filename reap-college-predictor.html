About REAP College Predictor

The REAP College Predictor is an offline-capable browser application designed to assist engineering aspirants in Rajasthan in estimating their admission options. Utilizing historical cutoffs, category adjustments, and quota structures, the tool performs immediate calculation of B.Tech options across major institutions in the state.

How REAP Admission Works

  • Admission Basis: Admission counseling is primarily conducted based on JEE Main percentiles, followed by Class 12th PCM marks for remaining vacant seats.
  • Domicile Rule: 85% of total seats in government and private institutions are reserved for Rajasthan Domicile candidates. The remaining 15% seats are open to Out of Rajasthan candidates.
  • GAS vs SFS Seats: Government institutions offer two seat categories. GAS (Government Aided Seats) have highly subsidized fee packages, whereas SFS (Self-Financed Seats) carry moderate fee levels.

How to Use the Predictor

  1. Select your entry option: JEE Main Score or 12th PCM %.
  2. Input your score, domicile, category, gender, and preferred college type.
  3. Click Predict My Admission Chances to view a custom breakdown of options.
  4. Filter by branch or admission chance, compare up to 3 colleges, or generate your choice filling recommendation.

Key Features

  • Dynamic Calculations: Accounts for category offsets, female reservations, and Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) cutoffs.
  • Comparison Matrix: Select colleges to compare fees, placement statistics, and pros/cons side-by-side.
  • Choice Filling Assistant: Generates a structured priority list to maximize your allotment probability.
  • Private & Safe: Performs calculations locally on your device. No user profile data is uploaded.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the predicted seat guaranteed?
No, allotments are determined by the counseling board based on live registrations and choices locked in the current year. This tool provides indicative assessments based on historical trends.

What is the TFWS seat category?
Tuition Fee Waiver Scheme (TFWS) represents 5% supernumerary seats for high-merit candidates whose annual family income is below ₹8.0 Lakhs. TFWS seats waive the tuition fee portion, leaving only minimal development/utility charges.

Does the gender setting affect chances?
Yes, Rajasthan engineering institutions reserve horizontal supernumerary quotas for female aspirants. This generally results in a slightly lower cutoff threshold for female candidates.
