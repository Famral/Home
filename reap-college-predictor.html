About the REAP College Predictor 2026

The REAP College Predictor is a free, data-driven tool that helps Rajasthan B.Tech aspirants estimate their admission chances across 13+ engineering colleges based on historical REAP cutoff data.

Dual Score Entry Modes

Enter either your JEE Main Percentile or your Class 12th PCM percentage to get predictions. The tool maps both score types against historical REAP cutoff data to deliver accurate college-branch match results for 2026 admissions.

Official Cutoff Database

Built on verified cutoff data covering all 13 major Rajasthan engineering colleges including MBM Jodhpur, CTAE Udaipur, RTU Kota, SKIT Jaipur, JECRC, Arya College, and more. Cutoffs are maintained for every branch, category (UR/OBC/SC/ST/MBC/EWS), domicile, and TFWS quota combination.

Category & Domicile Aware

The predictor applies the correct cutoff adjustments for your reservation category (General, OBC, MBC, SC, ST, EWS) and domicile state (Rajasthan Home State Quota vs. All India Category). Female applicants also get gender-based seat consideration for applicable colleges.

Placement & Rating Insights

Each predicted college card shows average and highest placement figures (LPA), college rating, established year, and city — so you can evaluate colleges beyond just cutoffs. Expand any card to view detailed pros, cons, fees, and seat type (GAS/SFS) information at a glance.

Side-by-Side College Comparison

Add up to 3 college-branch combinations to the comparison tray using the Compare button on any result card. The comparison drawer shows location, fees, placements, star rating, REAP cutoff, and detailed pros & cons side-by-side to help you make an informed final decision.

Rajasthan B.Tech Admission & REAP Cutoff Insights

Securing admission into Rajasthan's top engineering universities requires a clear understanding of the REAP portal guidelines, reservation policies, and previous cutoff trends.

Top Rajasthan Engineering Colleges

Rajasthan has a mix of prestigious government university departments and high-performing private colleges:

  • MBM University, Jodhpur: Formerly MBM Engineering College, it is one of the oldest and most respected engineering institutes in India. CSE, IT, and ECE branches have very competitive cutoffs, often closing under 70,000 CRL.
  • CTAE, Udaipur: College of Technology and Engineering is highly acclaimed for technical disciplines. Located on a lush green campus, it's popular for low fees and quality academics.
  • SKIT & JECRC, Jaipur: Leading private institutions that are highly preferred by candidates for their robust placement records, modern infrastructure, and prime location in Jaipur.

REAP Seat Allocation Factors

Cutoff ranks vary dynamically during counseling. Keep these primary factors in mind:

  • JEE Main CRL AIR Rank: REAP uses All India CRL ranks for seat allocation. Category ranks are used as filters under the specific reservation quotas rather than the category-specific merit positions.
  • Preference Checklist: Place your dream colleges at the top of your list. The Choice-Filling Basket on this page is designed to help you construct a structured preference list to copy-paste during official REAP registration.
  • TFWS Eligibility: Make sure your income certificate is issued and verified correctly prior to REAP counseling, confirming your family's annual income is below ₹8 Lakhs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the predicted seat guaranteed?
No, allotments are determined by the counseling board based on live registrations and choices locked in the current year. This tool provides indicative assessments based on historical trends.
What is the TFWS seat category?
Tuition Fee Waiver (TFWS) represents 5% supernumerary seats for high-merit candidates whose annual family income is below ₹8.0 Lakhs. TFWS seats waive the tuition fee portion, leaving only minimal development/utility charges.
Does the gender setting affect chances?
Yes, Rajasthan engineering institutions reserve horizontal supernumerary quotas for female aspirants. This generally results in a slightly lower cutoff threshold for female candidates.
